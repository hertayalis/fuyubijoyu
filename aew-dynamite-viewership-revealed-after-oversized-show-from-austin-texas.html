AEW Dynamite Viewership Revealed After Oversized Show From Austin, Texas

AEW Dynamite is still going strong, with Double or Nothing on the horizon on May 28th. They also presented a very appealing show in Austin, Texas this week, complete with a steel cage main event match, but how did the viewership turn out?

Showbuzz Daily reports that the May 17th episode from Austin, Texas brought an overnight average of 814,000 viewers, with a .28 in the 18 to 49 demographic.

The May 10th episode of AEW Dynamite brought an overnight average of 877,000 viewers from Detroit, Michigan. They also scored a .32 in the 18 to 49 demographic.

The May 3th episode of AEW Dynamite brought in an overnight average of 776,000 viewers, with a .28 in the 18 to 49 demographic.

April 26th’s episode of AEW Dynamite brought in an overnight average of 863,000 viewers, with a .28 rating in the 18 to 49 demographic.

The April 19th episode of AEW Dynamite brought in an overnight average of 830,000 viewers, with a .28 in the 18 to 49 demographic.

866,000 fans tuned in to watch AEW Dynamite’s April 12th episode. That brought in a .28 in the 18 to 49 demographic as well.

The April 5th episode of AEW Dynamite brought in an overnight average of 877,000 viewers, with a .3 in the 18 to 49 demographic.

March 29th episode of AEW Dynamite brought an overnight average of 833,000 viewers, with a .28 in the 18 to 49 demographic.

March 22nd’s episode of AEW Dynamite brought an overnight average of 954,000 viewers, with a .33 in the 18 to 49 demographic.

The March 15th episode of AEW Dynamite brought an overnight average viewership of 852,000, with a .27 in the 18 to 49 demographic.

March 8th episode of AEW Dynamite brought an overnight average of 858,000 viewers, with a .29 in the 18 to 49 demographic.

The March 1st episode of AEW Dynamite brought in an average overnight audience of 833,000 viewers. They scored a .27 in the 18 to 49 demographic. This was a big drop in viewership after so many tuned in for Tony Khan’s big announcement.
